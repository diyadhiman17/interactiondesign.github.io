• the importance of female friendships

    having a strong circle of female friends is like holding onto a life raft in a sea of chaos. these are the relationships that will remind you who you are, even when you forget.

  • move away from your hometown

    staying where you grew up feels safe, but leaving forces you to grow in ways you never imagined. trust me, distance has a funny way of giving you perspective.

  • do not cut your own fringe

    you think you can do it. you can’t. leave the DIY bangs for Pinterest fails and let a professional handle the scissors.

  • fall back in love with your childhood hobbies

    remember that thing you used to love doing before life got complicated? it’s still there, waiting for you to rediscover it. Go on, indulge in a little nostalgia.

  • document everything

    you think you’ll remember it all, but you won’t. write it down, take photos, keep notes. future-you will thank you for capturing the chaos.

  • you don’t have to follow every passion to its end

    it’s okay to start things and not finish them. life isn’t a race to turn every hobby into a career—sometimes it’s just about enjoying the ride.

  • the myth of work-life balance

    work-life balance is less a reality and more of a juggling act where you’re always dropping something. the key is to pick up what matters and let the rest go.

  • say no more often

    turns out, "no" is a complete sentence. saying yes to everything is a fast track to burnout, so learn the power of a polite (or not so polite) refusal.

  • don’t mix alcohol and weed

    This isn’t one of those lessons you need to learn the hard way. Whether you’re going out or staying in, pick one vice at a time—your body will thank you.

  • being able to do things alone is a necessity

    if you can’t take yourself out to dinner or go to the movies solo, you’re missing out on one of life’s greatest freedoms: independence. learn to enjoy your own company.

  • your parents are people, not just your parents

    it’s a hard pill to swallow, but your parents had lives, dreams, and mistakes long before you showed up. understanding this can change your relationship with them.

  • life won’t always give you closure

    sometimes, you have to create your own closure. whether it's a breakup, a lost opportunity, or an unfinished chapter, it’s up to you to move forward without waiting for the perfect ending.
